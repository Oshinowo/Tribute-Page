Nelson Mandela


The Man Who Saved The Black Race

Nelson Mandela - former president of south africa

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born into the Madiba clan in the village of Mvezo, in the Eastern Cape
  • 1930 - When he was 12 years old, his father died and the young Rolihlahla became a ward of Jongintaba at the Great Place in Mqhekezweni
  • 1941 - He worked as a mine security officer and after meeting Walter Sisulu, an estate agent, he was introduced to Lazer Sidelsky
  • 1943 - He completed his BA through the University of South Africa and went back to Fort Hare for his graduation
  • 1952 - By his own admission he was a poor student and left the university without graduating.
  • 1962 - He only started studying again through the University of London after his imprisonment but also did not complete that degree.
  • 1989 - while in the last months of his imprisonment, he obtained an LLB through the University of South Africa. He graduated in absentia at a ceremony in Cape Town.
  • 1944 - Mandela, while increasingly politically involved from 1942, only joined the African National Congress when he helped to form the ANC Youth League (ANCYL).
  • 1944 - he married Walter Sisulu’s cousin, Evelyn Mase, a nurse. They had two sons, Madiba Thembekile "Thembi" and Makgatho, and two daughters both called Makaziwe, the first of whom died in infancy
  • 1958 - He and his wife divorced in 1958.
  • 1949 - Mandela rose through the ranks of the ANCYL and through its efforts, the ANC adopted a more radical mass-based policy, the Programme of Action
  • 1952 - He was chosen as the National Volunteer-in-Chief of the Defiance Campaign with Maulvi Cachalia as his deputy
  • 1993 - He and President FW de Klerk jointly won the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1994 - He was inaugurated as South Africa’s first democratically elected President.
  • 1998 - On his 80th birthday, he married Graça Machel, his third wife.
  • 2013 - He died at his home in Johannesburg.

“ I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to achieve. But if needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die. ”
